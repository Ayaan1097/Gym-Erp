What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measurement that uses your height and weight to determine if your weight is healthy. It provides an estimate of body fat and helps assess health risks associated with weight.

BMI Limitations

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it has some limitations:

  • Doesn't account for muscle mass
  • May not be accurate for athletes or muscular individuals
  • Doesn't measure body fat distribution
  • May not be as accurate for elderly individuals

BMI FAQs

It's generally recommended to check your BMI every 3-6 months, or whenever you experience significant weight changes.

The BMI calculation formula is the same for both men and women. However, men typically have more muscle mass than women, which may affect the interpretation of results.
